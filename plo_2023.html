Informatics Undergraduate
Programmatic Learning Outcomes

Draft April 7, 2023
Informatics Undergraduate Curriculum Committee
📌 These are the 2023 faculty-approved PLOs, reproduced here with their original language and structure. Each of the eight categories was extended with additional sub-outcomes in Fall 2025 — those extended versions are used in the CLO mapping tools.
Program Goal

Upon completion of the Informatics BS degree students will be able to evaluate and create solutions to problems using a combination of sociotechnical, computational, and design thinking.

Computational Thinking

A problem-solving methodology that involves breaking down complex problems into smaller, more manageable parts and using mathematical and algorithmic thinking to develop solutions.

Sociotechnical Thinking

A way of understanding, designing, managing, and evaluating systems that considers both the social and technical aspects of the system, as well as the interdependence of these aspects.

Design Thinking

A human-centered approach to problem-solving that emphasizes empathy, creativity, collaboration, and iteration to envision, prototype, and revise solutions.

While presented in eight categories, the programmatic learning outcomes listed above are not wholly separate domains; rather, each informs and shapes the other.

We recognize that the Informatics curriculum is just one part of a student's educational experience at IU and within Luddy and that their education will be informed, and enriched by university general education, cognates, minors, and elective courses.

Programmatic Learning Outcomes
Knowledge

Students will demonstrate knowledge of key concepts, principles, and theories in Informatics, including ethics, design, data structures and algorithms, human-computer interaction, databases, sociotechnical analysis, and information systems development.

➕ Extended Fall 2025
Technical Skills

Students will develop skills in applying knowledge to design, develop, and implement information systems and applications using a wide variety of programming languages, frameworks, prototyping, and development tools.

➕ Extended Fall 2025
Analytical Skills

Students will be able to analyze and interpret systems and data in context using statistical, computational, and critical theories. They will also be able to use qualitative and information literacy methods to draw conclusions and make decisions.

➕ Extended Fall 2025
Problem-solving Skills

Students will be able to identify, decompose, and solve complex problems in various domains using sociotechnical, computational, and design methodologies to create innovative, sustainable, and equitable solutions for real-world problems.

➕ Extended Fall 2025
Communication

Students will be able to communicate effectively to diverse audiences.

➕ Extended Fall 2025
Collaboration

Students will be able to work collaboratively in interdisciplinary teams to develop and implement solutions to complex problems, and work effectively with and within diverse groups.

➕ Extended Fall 2025
Ethics

Students will be able to identify ethical concerns and dilemmas so they can understand and apply ethical principles and practices in the local and global design and use of technologies.

➕ Extended Fall 2025
Lifelong Learning

Students will be able to engage in self-directed learning and professional development to recognize and engage with emerging trends, technologies, skills, and practices in order to adapt to changing contexts and challenges.

➕ Extended Fall 2025
