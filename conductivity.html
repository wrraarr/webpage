How Electrons Behave

Conductor (Gold/Copper)

Electrons move freely and quickly through the material. The atomic structure allows electrons to flow with minimal resistance, making excellent electrical conductors.

Semiconductor (Silicon)

Electrons move moderately with some resistance. The behavior can be controlled by adding impurities (doping), making them perfect for electronic devices.

Insulator (Rubber/Glass)

Electrons are tightly bound to atoms and barely move. This prevents electrical current from flowing, making them excellent for safety and protection.
