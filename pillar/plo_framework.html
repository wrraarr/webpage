Program Learning Outcomes

The Official PLO Framework organizes the Informatics BS program learning outcomes into eight independent competency categories — this is the faculty-approved structure. The Pillar-Based Mapper is an alternative visualization that uses a three-pillar organization (Computational, Sociotechnical, Design) generated when the original mapping tool was first built; it was not part of the faculty-approved framework.

Use this page to explore both organizational structures, see how they compare, and choose the right tool for your course.

Framework Crosswalk

How the pillar-based mapper's three-pillar structure maps conceptually to the official 8-category PLO framework. The official framework expands the PLOs into eight independent competency domains — some map closely to a single pillar, while others (Ethics, Collaboration, Communication) draw from multiple pillars. Note: this is a conceptual mapping between two organizational schemes, not between two different sets of program outcomes.


2025 Category Closest 2023 Pillar(s) Conceptual Relationship
Core Knowledge Computational Thinking + Sociotechnical Thinking Foundation content across all three pillars; explicitly names domains of knowledge (systems, HCI, ethics, policy) that were implicit in the 2023 framework.
Technical Practice Computational Thinking Closely mirrors the "Effectively Implement" and "Apply Algorithms" categories. Expands to include cloud, APIs, accessibility, and prototyping tools.
Data & Analysis Computational Thinking Expands "Curate Data" and "Reason Logically" into a broader analytical competency that includes both quantitative and qualitative methods.
Problem Solving Computational Thinking + Design Thinking Bridges the computational and design pillars. Adds explicit emphasis on sustainability, scale, and stakeholder constraints absent from the 2023 framework.
Communication Sociotechnical Thinking + Design Thinking Was distributed across "Create Solutions" and "Disseminate" in 2023. The 2025 framework elevates communication to a standalone competency with written, oral, visual, and cross-cultural dimensions.
Collaboration Sociotechnical Thinking + Design Thinking Was split across "Create Solutions" and "Collaborate" categories. The 2025 framework is more explicit about conflict resolution, role negotiation, and project management.
Ethics & Responsibility Sociotechnical Thinking Directly maps to "Ethical Sensibility" and "Analyze Solutions." The 2025 version is more concrete, naming algorithmic bias, environmental sustainability, and professional codes as distinct outcomes.
Lifelong Learning New in 2025 Entirely new category with no direct equivalent in 2023. Captures metacognitive competencies — self-directed learning, critical evaluation of technology, and professional development — previously not addressed.
