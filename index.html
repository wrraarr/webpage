How It Works

  1. Enter your CLOs Open the Data Entry tool. Add your course number, title, and a short description. Then add each Course Learning Outcome — these are the specific things students will be able to do after completing your course.
  2. Link CLOs to PLOs For each CLO, click through the three pillars (Computational, Sociotechnical, Design) to browse PLO categories. Click the PLO chips that your CLO aligns with. Hover on any chip to see the full PLO description. Most CLOs map to 1–4 PLOs.
  3. Review and send Click "Send to Viewer" to see a summary of what you're about to submit. The preview shows warnings for any incomplete entries. Confirm, and the mapper opens with your courses loaded automatically.
  4. Explore the map In the mapper, your courses appear as nodes connected to PLOs through their CLOs. Click nodes to expand/collapse, drag to rearrange, and right-click to pin. Use the "PLO Coverage" button to see a histogram of which PLOs are well-covered and which have gaps.

The Three Pillars

Our program's PLOs are organized into three pillars, each containing categories of specific learning outcomes.

Computational Thinking

Reason logically, curate data, apply algorithms, and effectively implement solutions. 22 PLOs covering programming, data structures, system design, and security.

Sociotechnical Thinking

Ethical sensibility, analyzing solutions, and creating solutions. 17 PLOs covering stakeholder analysis, values-based decisions, and responsible design.

Design Thinking

Research, design, evaluate, disseminate, and collaborate. 23 PLOs covering HCI methods, prototyping, user personas, and team-based design.

JSON Format Reference

If you prefer to write JSON directly (or use the GPT to generate it), here's the expected format. PLO IDs follow the pattern pillar-category-number.

{ "courses": [ { "courseNumber": "INFO-I 101", "courseTitle": "Introduction to Informatics", "description": "A ~50-word course description...", "clos": [ { "text": "Decompose real-world problems into sub-problems", "ploMappings": ["comp-reason-3", "comp-reason-4"] } ] } ] }

Tips for Good Mappings

  1. Start with action verbs Write CLOs that begin with measurable verbs: analyze, construct, evaluate, design, implement. This makes it easier to identify which PLOs align.
  2. Aim for 1–4 PLO mappings per CLO If a CLO maps to more than 4 PLOs, it might be too broad. Consider splitting it into more specific outcomes. If it maps to zero, it may not align with program goals.
  3. Check cross-pillar coverage Many courses naturally touch multiple pillars. An implementation-focused course might also have sociotechnical or design components — don't overlook those connections.
  4. Use the histogram After loading your courses, open the PLO Coverage panel. Look for PLOs with zero hits (gaps in your course) and PLOs with unusually high counts (potential over-emphasis).
